**Baidarka**
Are you familiar with [George Dyson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Dyson_(science_historian))? He's in Bellingham WA now. George spent many years designing and building these incredible cloth-on-aluminum-frame kayaks, based on Aleut construction techniques modified by Russians in the 1800s to be more stable and able to take on cargo. He sold kits for these in the 90s and wrote a [lovely book](https://www.amazon.com/Baidarka-Kayak-George-Dyson/dp/088240315X) on history and construction techniques...

Here's a [build log](http://gdyson.com/a-builders-journal/) for one of these amazing boats, and a photo:

**Quantum Experiments**
I recently found two lovely books that teach quantum mechanics using a sequence of hobbyist-level builds of experimental setups for the big "strange" discoveries over the years that really broke with classical mechanics:

- [Discovering the Quantum](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1980450374/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_search_asin_title?ie=UTF8&amp;psc=1)
- [Exploring Quantum Physics through Hands On Projects](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1118140664/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_search_asin_title?ie=UTF8&amp;psc=1)

The first book builds up to a nuclear magnetic resonance imager... it's a WILD project, but I think I can make it far more approachable for people (and myself) by handling all of the fiddly bits with high tolerance using the printer and cutter.

**Floor Turtle**
As noted above, cloning the old floor turtle, and hooking it up to the ODE solver in my computer algebra system, is a must. That goes beyond CAD into interactive computing with the microcontroller.

Of course I also need to build some way to export paths to the laser cutter from that ODE solver, so I can etch Lorenz attractors into stuff around the house.

**Fairy Houses**
My daughter and I are going to design little fairy houses to plant around the yard and forest, [in this style](https://www.amazon.com/Fairy-Houses-Create-Whimsical-Homes/dp/1591866723/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1DWLEV5SYOL7D&amp;keywords=fairy+houses&amp;qid=1658147443&amp;s=books&amp;sprefix=fairy+houses%2Cstripbooks%2C118&amp;sr=1-1)... she is stoked on Norse Mythology now, thanks to Neil Gaiman's illustrated editions, and REALLY wants Thor's Hammer. So I guess small-scale LARPing is now on the table??
